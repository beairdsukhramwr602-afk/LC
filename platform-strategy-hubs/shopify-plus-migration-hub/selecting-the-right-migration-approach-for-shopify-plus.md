# Selecting the Right Migration Approach for Shopify Plus

Choosing the right migration approach for Shopify Plus is less about how much data the store contains and more about how much commercial meaning needs to survive the move without distortion. Shopify Plus can support more native business structure than standard Shopify, especially around B2B relationships, account access, catalogs, and broader store governance. That makes the platform powerful, but it also makes approach selection more sensitive to unclear target design.

The practical question is not which service level moves records faster. The real question is which level of support gives the business the best chance of preserving the outcomes that matter after launch. For Shopify Plus, those outcomes often include company-based buying behavior, catalog-controlled pricing and access, account continuity, store-boundary logic, high-value storefront paths, and the extension-driven behavior that still sits outside the native core.

A useful way to think about the decision is simple: the safer migration approach is the one that matches both the store’s structural complexity and the team’s ability to make confident target decisions, review results carefully, and catch commercial risk early.

### Start with a Demo Migration, not with assumptions

A Shopify Plus project should not choose its migration approach only from platform ambition, store size, or internal confidence. The fastest reliable way to judge the right path is to run a Demo Migration built around the parts of the store most likely to expose structural weakness.

For Shopify Plus, a high-signal sample usually includes:

* representative companies and company locations
* B2B customers with different access, pricing, or account conditions
* products where catalog control and product complexity intersect
* one-store or multi-store scenarios that reveal boundary decisions
* app-dependent or custom-data-dependent behavior that affects buying or operations
* the storefront, account, and URL paths that carry the most commercial risk

The purpose of the demo is not broad coverage. It is early clarity. A small number of well-chosen scenarios usually reveals more about the right migration approach than a large volume of low-risk data.

### Standard Migration Service

#### When Standard Migration Service is usually sufficient

Standard Migration Service is often suitable when the Shopify Plus target structure is already clear and the store’s most important behavior can be expressed through the platform without special handling.

This is usually the case when:

* the business has already defined its company, company-location, and catalog model clearly
* the decision between blended and separate-store architecture is already settled
* customer-account expectations are understood well enough to plan continuity confidently
* app dependence is limited for revenue-critical behavior, or the dependent outcomes are well understood and acceptable
* the team can review results carefully and make sound validation decisions without needing expert-led execution

Standard Migration Service is usually a good fit when the store behaves like a well-defined Shopify Plus case rather than a structurally uncertain one.

#### What demo results usually support a Standard decision

A Standard decision becomes more credible when the demo shows that:

* company and location relationships remain understandable
* catalog-controlled access and pricing behave as intended
* complex products still produce clear sellable outcomes
* account-access expectations remain workable
* store-boundary choices feel coherent rather than improvised
* any gaps are limited, explainable, and unlikely to require tailored handling

The key sign is not perfection. It is clarity. Standard Migration Service is strongest when the remaining issues are narrow enough that the team can resolve them through normal review and validation without needing a more guided path.

### Managed Migration Service

#### When Managed Migration Service is the safer choice

Managed Migration Service is often the safer path when the target model appears viable, but the business wants expert-led execution to reduce ambiguity, coordination burden, or the risk of poor interpretation during migration and review.

This is often the case when:

* the Shopify Plus structure is mostly understood, but the team wants a more controlled end-to-end process
* B2B relationships, catalogs, pricing visibility, or store architecture require careful interpretation even if they do not appear to need custom transformation
* multiple internal reviewers are involved and the business needs a more structured review flow
* validation workload is large enough that expert guidance would reduce avoidable uncertainty
* SEO-sensitive paths, account continuity, or multi-store decisions increase launch risk even though the target remains broadly compatible

Managed Migration Service is not about removing the need for validation. It is about making execution and validation more reliable when the business would benefit from stronger expert guidance.

#### What demo results usually support a Managed decision

A Managed decision is often the right one when the demo is mostly encouraging, but still reveals decisions that require judgment rather than simple confirmation.

Typical signals include:

* B2B structure looks viable, but catalog or account logic still needs expert interpretation
* product behavior is mostly correct, but some commercially important cases need closer review
* multi-store or blended-store decisions are workable, but not yet fully confident
* app-owned behavior exists and needs classification, even if it may not require custom transformation
* the business can see a clean target path, but does not want execution risk to depend entirely on internal bandwidth

In practice, Managed Migration Service is often the safest choice when the business is not facing a fundamentally incompatible target, but also should not rely on a do-it-yourself interpretation of a more complex Shopify Plus structure.

### Custom Migration Service

#### When Custom Migration Service is the safest choice

Custom Migration Service is usually the safest choice when standard handling cannot preserve the intended outcome without custom treatment. In Shopify Plus, this often happens when the platform is viable in principle, but preserving business meaning depends on transformations, non-standard mapping, filtered logic, or special handling beyond the default migration path.

This is often the case when:

* important B2B behavior depends on structure that needs custom transformation
* app-owned logic carries revenue-critical meaning that cannot weaken safely
* customer-account outcomes depend on non-standard identity, access, or membership rules
* the business needs selective migration handling based on conditions rather than simple entity transfer
* legacy enterprise behavior must be re-expressed carefully to fit the current Shopify Plus model
* the store appears compatible at a high level, but the details that matter most do not align cleanly through standard handling

Custom Migration Service is the right path when the target can work, but only if the migration is tailored deliberately enough to preserve the business outcome.

#### What demo results usually support a Custom decision

A Custom decision is often supported when the demo reveals that the target does not fail completely, but also does not preserve the required result through standard logic.

Typical signals include:

* the right records appear, but the wrong customers receive the wrong access or pricing
* company and location structure needs transformation rather than simple mapping
* app-driven or custom-data-driven meaning is necessary for revenue, operations, or customer continuity
* the business cannot define an acceptable launch result without tailored rules
* structurally important edge cases are too central to defer safely until after migration

This is also the point where a Custom Job may become necessary as part of a broader Custom Migration path, especially when specific behaviors need bespoke handling to preserve meaning accurately.

### How to choose between Standard, Managed, and Custom

A practical way to separate the three paths is to ask three questions.

First, is the target model already clear enough that the business can explain how customers, companies, catalogs, store boundaries, and account access should work after launch?

Second, did the Demo Migration show that the important scenarios behave correctly, or did it show outcomes that still need expert interpretation?

Third, if important outcomes are still not preserved, is the problem mainly execution burden, or does it require tailored handling?

The answers usually point in this direction:

* choose **Standard Migration Service** when the structure is clear, the demo is clean enough, and the team can review confidently
* choose **Managed Migration Service** when the structure is mostly viable, but the business wants expert-led execution and a more reliable review process
* choose **Custom Migration Service** when preserving the intended outcome requires tailored handling beyond standard logic

The best choice is not the most advanced service by default. It is the service model that matches the real complexity the demo reveals.

### Account for customer continuity, store architecture, and high-value paths in the decision

A Shopify Plus migration approach should not be chosen only from catalog structure. Three other areas often change the safer path.

Customer continuity can push the decision upward when the business needs a more controlled first-login and account-access experience across business-customer contexts.

Store architecture can push the decision upward when the business is still deciding between blended and separate-store models, or when different storefront contexts carry different commercial responsibilities.

High-value storefront and SEO paths can also influence the decision when the business depends on a small set of commercially important domains, landing paths, product routes, or market-sensitive entry points behaving correctly after launch.

These factors do not automatically require a more tailored service model, but they often increase the value of expert-led review when the business impact of getting them wrong is high.

### Conclusion

The right Shopify Plus migration approach is the one that matches the real complexity of the target, not the one that looks sufficient on paper before the target has been tested. Standard Migration Service is often enough when the Shopify Plus structure is already well defined and the demo confirms that important behavior remains intact. Managed Migration Service is safer when the target looks viable but the business needs stronger expert-led execution and review. Custom Migration Service is the right choice when preserving the intended outcome depends on tailored handling beyond the standard path.

A practical next step is to run a Demo Migration using the scenarios most likely to expose structural weakness early, then judge the result against clear commercial pass conditions.
