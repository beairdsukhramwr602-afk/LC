# How to Use the Learning Center

Most eCommerce platform migrations go off track for predictable reasons: unclear scope, underestimated complexity, rushed validation, weak platform-fit analysis, and unclear responsibility across teams. The Learning Center is designed to reduce those risks by helping you find the right guidance for the decision you are trying to make.

If you do only one thing at the start, define what must remain true after launch for your store. That single step improves every decision that follows: platform choice, migration path planning, service model selection, validation priorities, and launch readiness.

The Learning Center is decision-stage guidance for professionals planning or evaluating an e-commerce data migration. It focuses on:

* scope definition and cost drivers
* platform differences that affect migration outcomes
* service model choices that affect execution responsibility and handling depth
* risk signals that change the safest migration approach
* validation priorities that help prevent launch surprises

It is not setup documentation, credential guidance, troubleshooting material, or a technical repair manual.

### The fastest way to get value

Use the Learning Center as a decision tool, not as a library to read from beginning to end.

#### 1. Define what must remain true after launch

Start with the business outcomes that cannot be broken during the move.

Examples include:

* purchase-critical product behavior
* category and navigation logic
* customer recognition and account expectations
* order history continuity
* SEO continuity for your most important pages

This step gives the rest of the project a clear standard for what success should look like.

#### 2. Validate assumptions early

A Demo Migration is most useful when it includes the highest-risk part of your store, not the easiest part.

That usually means:

* complex products
* top categories or high-value browse paths
* order and customer scenarios that matter to support, reporting, or repeat purchasing
* workflows that drive revenue and day-to-day operations

Early evidence is far more useful than late reassurance.

#### 3. Use evidence to choose the right approach

The goal is not only to confirm that data moved. The goal is to confirm that the migrated store can still support the outcomes your business depends on.

When you review results, focus on whether the migration output is structurally usable, operationally meaningful, and ready for deeper validation before launch.

### Recommended reading paths by goal

Start with **Migration Fundamentals**.

Focus first on readiness, risk framing, and the practical meaning of migration scope. Once those basics are clear, move into **Strategy and Planning for Migration** so you can judge likely effort, validation workload, and decision points before committing to a Target Platform or service provider.

#### If you already decided to migrate and need to choose a safe approach <a href="#if-you-already-decided-to-migrate-and-need-to-choose-a-safe-approach" id="if-you-already-decided-to-migrate-and-need-to-choose-a-safe-approach"></a>

Use Strategy and Planning for scope, complexity, decision structure, timeline logic, and validation planning. Then use Next-Cart Migration Service to understand Demo Migration, Entity Points, Recent Data Migration, Re-Migration, Add-ons, service model choice, and Custom Service requirements.

#### If you are choosing a Target Platform <a href="#if-you-are-choosing-a-target-platform" id="if-you-are-choosing-a-target-platform"></a>

Go to the **Platform Strategy Hubs**.

Use them to understand how each Target Platform differs in structure, where constraints are likely to appear, and what should be validated first for that specific platform.

#### If you are planning the project timeline <a href="#if-you-are-planning-the-project-timeline" id="if-you-are-planning-the-project-timeline"></a>

Use **Strategy and Planning for Migration** together with **Post-Migration and Quality Assurance**.

These sections help you define milestones, decision points, validation stages, acceptance criteria, go-live judgment, and post-launch stabilization so review work does not get compressed into the final days before launch.

### Conclusion

The most reliable migration plans are built on early clarity and early evidence. When you define what must remain true after launch, identify complexity early, and validate representative samples before committing too deeply, scope decisions become clearer and outcomes become more predictable.

If you are still defining scope and success criteria, start with **Migration Fundamentals**. If you already understand the fundamentals and need to shape the project path, move next into **Strategy and Planning for Migration** and **Next-Cart Migration Service**. If you have already chosen a Target Platform and need platform-specific risk signals, use the relevant **Platform Strategy Hub** next, then return to planning and validation content where needed.

Run a Demo Migration early to reveal hidden complexity in products, customers, orders, catalog structure, and SEO-critical content. If the result shows standard handling is not enough, use Live Chat to clarify service fit, Add-on needs, Custom Service requirements, and the safest migration path before full execution.

### FAQs

**Should I read platform hub content before Migration Fundamentals?**

Usually, no. Start with Migration Fundamentals if you are still defining scope, risk, or success criteria. Platform hub content becomes more useful once you are comparing or leaning toward a specific Target Platform.

**How do I know which articles apply to my role?**

Start with the reading path that matches your role or immediate decision, then adjust based on what you need to evaluate next. For example, someone responsible for timelines should prioritize planning, Next-Cart service understanding, and validation content, while someone responsible for platform choice or integrations should prioritize platform differences and technical deep dives.

**Should I read the Next-Cart Migration Service section before choosing a service model?**

Yes. Use that section to understand what Next-Cart provides, what the selected migration path means, how Demo Migration and Entity Points support planning, and how Standard Service, Managed Service, and Custom Service differ. That context makes service model selection safer and less assumption-driven.
